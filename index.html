About

I grew up in the small city of Surat in India and have lived in India, Thailand, and the United States, each shaping my academic journey. I pursued my B.S. in Physics with a Specialization in Astrophysics at the University of California, San Diego. Early on, I developed a strong interest in computational methods and data science, which led me to explore research in galaxy formation, dark matter modeling, and the application of machine learning to astrophysical problems. I hope to continue researching the intersection of astronomy and computational methods as I pursue a PhD.

Throughout my academic journey, I have worked on various topics, including stellar stream dynamics and dark matter subhalos, supermassive black hole outflows, and the interstellar medium in star-forming galaxies. My experience spans deep learning, probabilistic modeling, and large-scale data analysis, equipping me with the skills to tackle complex astrophysical questions.

Research

My research focuses on computational astrophysics, galaxy formation, dark matter modeling, and the application of machine learning to astrophysical problems. I am particularly interested in leveraging modern data-driven methodologies and numerical simulations to uncover the fundamental physics governing the cosmos.

1. Accelerating Gravitational Wave Astronomy with Machine Learning (Summer 2025 – Present)

Advisor: Prof. Erik Katsavounidis, MIT

I focus on analyzing gravitational-wave data from LIGO detectors to identify astrophysical sources and enhance the sensitivity of searches using AI and Machine Learning methods.

2. Stellar Streams, Dark Matter Substructure, and Machine Learning (Summer 2023 – Summer 2025)

Advisors: Prof. Javier Duarte and Prof. Tongyan Lin, UC San Diego

Stellar streams—tidally stretched remnants of dwarf galaxies and globular clusters—are powerful probes of dark matter substructure. I developed and implemented Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) methods to recover subhalo impact parameters and trained Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) for simulation-based inference to accelerate parameter recovery. Thousands of simulated streams were generated and analyzed using high-performance computing resources at the San Diego Supercomputer Center.

Presented at the 243rd AAS Meeting.

3. Investigating Metal-Poor, Star-Forming Dwarf Galaxies as Analogs for the Early Universe (Summer 2022)

Advisor: Dr. Peter Senchyna, Carnegie Observatories

Used SOFIA integral field spectroscopy to analyze far-infrared [O III] and [C II] emission in metal-poor dwarf galaxies, exploring interstellar medium conditions analogous to those in early-universe galaxies. Found that local analogs show strong [C II] emission unlike high-redshift counterparts, suggesting structural ISM differences.

Presented at the Carnegie Summer Research Symposium and the 241st AAS Meeting.

4. Black Hole Outflows and Galaxy Evolution (Fall 2022)

Advisor: Prof. Alison Coil, UC San Diego

Analyzed AGN-driven outflows in nearby galaxies using spectral and kinematic modeling. Implemented multi-component spectral decomposition to characterize outflow kinematics and demonstrated that a three-component model is needed to accurately represent SMBH outflows, improving our understanding of AGN feedback processes.
